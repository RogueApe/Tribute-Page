"Story of my Grandfather"

A man who strive to survive that drifted South.

Here's a timeline of my Grandfather's life:

  • 1918 - Born in China Guang Dong province - Dabu.
  • 1927 - Force to leave his hometown due to civil war outbreak in China, causing hardlife. A nine-year-old him and his elder brother took a merchant ship and travel south to Malaysia.
  • 1928 - Both had arrived in Penang Island of Malaya, and his elder brother found a job as an apprentice for locksmith, while my grandfather went for school and work as part-timer in a groceries shop.
  • 1930 - My grandfather had to stop schooling due to his need to find more money to survive, and went for a full time job as an apprentice for shoemaker.
  • 1933 - He met the love of his life, my Grandmother. She is a daughter of a village chief which located at Teluk Kumbar Gertak Sangull in Penang.
  • 1936 - He propose marriage to my grandmother, but her family cruely rejected due to his poor background.
  • 1938 - He had acquired some skillset of shoe making, crafting throughtout the years of experience. The ambitious him was well prepared to set up his own shoe maker shop located in Georgetown, and his business had a great start.
  • 1939 - My grandfather marry his love - my grandmother. They build their own house and manage the shop business together and had their own family.
  • 1941 - Japanese Armies invaded Malaya Peninsula at December.
  • 1942 - The war had cause severe impact to his business, and some of the Japanese army had force my grandfather to offer them some tribute during their daily patrol on the street.
  • 1946 - His business had recover after the last troop of Japanese army had finally left Malaya and repatriated to Japan due to their lost in WW2.
  • 1947 - His elder brother left Penang and headed to Singapore for a job that offer a greater income and he had establish a family there.
  • 1950 - My father has born which is my grandfather fourth kid and the youngest son.
  • 1965 - The first Prime Minster of Malaysia visited his shop and my grandfather had make him a beautiful crafted custom-made Batik shoes.
  • 1966 - Praised by the Tunku Abdul Rahman himself and receives Antique Batik Shoes Award.
  • 1970 - Business was stable & strong. He started to help poor person during his free time by offering food & teached them some crafting skill to survive on their own.
  • 1975 - He adopted some orphan.
  • 1999 - died at the age of 81.
